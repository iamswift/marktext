# Growing Food in the City

Urban gardening has grown popular in recent years as more residents look for ways to grow their own food in small spaces, and community gardens have become gathering points where neighbors trade seeds, tools, and advice, turning empty lots into shared green spaces that benefit the whole block.

Many cities now offer small grants or free compost to residents who start a garden plot, which lowers the barrier for people who are curius about growing vegetables but hesitant to spend much money up front.

A well-planned garden can produce vegetables, herbs, and even a small amount of fruit throughout most of the growing season.
