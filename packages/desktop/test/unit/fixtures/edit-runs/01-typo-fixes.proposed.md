# A Short History of Printing

The invention of the printing press in the fifteenth century changed how knowledge was received and shared across Europe. Before this innovation, books had to be copied by hand, a slow and expensive process.

Johannes Gutenberg is widely credited with combining movable type and an oil-based ink to make mass production of texts possible. Printers across the continent definitely tried to imitate his workshop in Mainz.

Within decades, printing presses had spread to major cities, and the cost of books dropped dramatically. This shift occurred alongside a broader increase in literacy across the population.

Historians continue to debate how much the printing press accelerated the Reformation and the spread of scientific ideas, but few dispute that it fundamentally changed the way information moved through society.

The legacy of that first printing workshop is still visible today, which is why museums around the world preserve early printed books as prized artifacts.
